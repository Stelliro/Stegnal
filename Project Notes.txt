# **PROJECT UMBRA**

**Noise-Based Image Encoding & AI Decoding**
**CONFIDENTIAL — INTERNAL DISTRIBUTION ONLY**

**Prepared by:** Stelliro & Umbra
**Date:** 27 September 2025

---

### **Executive Summary**

Project Umbra investigates the feasibility of transmitting and recovering digital images through *structured noise* carriers. The objective is to make the signal appear indistinguishable from random noise to outsiders while enabling an AI-assisted decoder to reconstruct the original content with at least **50% fidelity** on images of approximately **1 MB** or larger.

This is a **proof-of-possibility** initiative. The goal is to demonstrate that “hidden structure” can be embedded within apparent static and reliably extracted by machine learning models — a foundation for future secure, low-probability-of-intercept communication systems.

---

### **Objectives**

1. Develop a software-based prototype to encode and decode images via noise-like signals.
2. Demonstrate ≥50% decoding success using AI-assisted reconstruction on 1 MB+ images.
3. Explore whether the inherent imperfections (“messy key”) in the noise channel can be leveraged as a reproducible, unique cryptographic element.

---

### **Scope of Work**

* **Phase I (Simulation):** Build a Python-based encoder/decoder pipeline. Inject synthetic noise and distortions; test AI denoiser models.
* **Phase II (AI Integration):** Train and evaluate autoencoders and generative models to reconstruct images from noisy carriers.
* **Phase III (Physical Testing):** Extend to ultrasonic or UWB radio paths using off-the-shelf transducers/SDRs.

---

### **Key Research Questions**

* Which physical layer is optimal for embedding structured noise: pseudo-random sequences, spread-spectrum bursts, pulse-position modulation, or chaotic waveforms?
* How large must the training set be for the AI to learn to decode reliably?
* What metric best captures “50% decoding success” — pixel similarity, perceptual similarity (SSIM), or task-level recognition?
* Can noise artifacts themselves be stabilized and used as a *unique* per-channel key?

---

### **Baseline Assumptions**

* Work begins entirely in simulation — no human or animal exposure.
* Test images will be at least 1024×1024 pixels (~1 MB, PNG/JPEG).
* Synthetic channel noise includes Gaussian, salt-and-pepper, jitter, and random frequency shifts.
* First AI model will be a simple convolutional autoencoder. Later models may include diffusion or transformer architectures.
* “Success” means recognizable reconstruction, not bit-perfect recovery.

---

### **Pipeline Overview**

**Encoding Stage:**

1. Source image (~1 MB).
2. Optional compression/encryption (JPEG + AES).
3. Modulation into noise-like stream (DSSS/UWB/PRN waveform).
4. Injection of synthetic channel noise and distortions.

**Decoding Stage (AI-assisted):**

1. Capture noisy signal.
2. Preprocess into feature windows (spectrograms, chip arrays, time slices).
3. Feed into AI decoder:

   * *v0:* Autoencoder trained on noisy/clean pairs.
   * *v1:* Generative model to fill in missing fine detail.
4. Evaluate output using SSIM, PSNR, and perceptual metrics.

---

### **Milestones**

* **M1:** Encoder/decoder pipeline functioning without AI (ECC + correlation).
* **M2:** Generate dataset of 1,000+ image/noise pairs.
* **M3:** Train baseline AI denoiser achieving ≥50% SSIM on held-out images.
* **M4:** Implement “messy key” experiments: stabilize noise artifacts as reproducible features.
* **M5:** Produce internal demonstration video showing noise → image decoding.

---

### **Stretch Goals**

* Increase image size to 5–10 MB.
* Introduce adversarial jamming in simulation.
* Evaluate diffusion and contrastive learning models for decoding.
* Conduct physical layer tests using ultrasonic transducers or SDR radios.
* Evaluate MP4 ↔ WAV ↔ MP4 translation pipelines for future audiovisual experiments.

---

### **Operational Principles**

* **Flexible Encoding.** Do not lock into one waveform; maintain plug-and-play modulation layers.
* **Model Agnostic.** Start small; allow migration to larger or different AI models.
* **Metric Fluidity.** Support multiple success metrics — pixel, perceptual, or task-level.
* **Security Realism.** “Noise” is not cryptography; combine with proper keys for real confidentiality.

---

### **Risks & Mitigations**

* **Signal Destruction.** Use ECC plus AI denoising in tandem.
* **Overfitting.** Randomize noise patterns and expand dataset to improve generalization.
* **Compute Bottlenecks.** Begin with lower resolutions/models; scale later.
* **Security Illusion.** Document clearly where cryptographic guarantees end.

---

* Benchmark reconstruction on 10 test images.

### Addendum — Project lineage (2026-06-20)

Several parallel working copies (V4/V5/V6 and an "UmbraFusion" scaffold) were
consolidated; the duplicates are archived in `TOP SECRET/Backups/` and their
docs preserved in `LEGACY_DOCUMENTS.md`. Worthwhile directions carried forward
from that exploration:

* **Learned decoder.** Replace the correlation/denoise decoder with a trained
  model (AE / VAE / UNet) — the M3 milestone above. UmbraFusion stubbed
  `models/` + `training/` for exactly this.
* **Large-image mapping.** Instead of building giant permutation-index arrays,
  map pixels ⇄ audio through a PRP (Feistel network) keyed by the shared seed,
  and support a temporary `scale < 1.0` downscale for huge inputs on limited RAM.
* **Stable round-trip API.** Keep the public contract small and symmetric:
  `image_to_wave(img, sr=...)` / `wave_to_image(wav, sr=..., target_size=(W,H))`,
  same `seed` both ways. (Today the equivalent lives in `umbra.audio` +
  `umbra.encoding`/`decoding`.)
* **Dataset tooling.** Curriculum auto-fetch + verified raster transcode with
  retry/truncation checks (archived under `UmbraFusion/umbra_io/`).
